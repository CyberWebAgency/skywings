Golden Triangle with Khajuraho and Varanasi Tour

Duration: 9 Nights / 10 Days

Tour Overview

This comprehensive 10-day tour combines the classic Golden Triangle route (Delhi, Agra, Jaipur) with two of India's most significant cultural destinations: Khajuraho and Varanasi. Experience the diverse heritage of India from the majestic Mughal architecture and Rajput splendor to the spiritual banks of the Ganges and the magnificent temple art of Khajuraho. This journey offers a perfect blend of history, culture, spirituality, and architectural marvels.

Tour Itinerary

Day 1: Delhi – Arrival

Arrive in Delhi, where you'll be greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel. Delhi, the capital of India, is a city steeped in history and legend, having attracted rulers from various dynasties throughout its glorious yet turbulent past. Though abandoned many times, its rulers returned to rebuild it at least seven times. Today, the twin cities of Old and New Delhi continue to fascinate visitors as the cultural and political capital of the world's largest democracy.

Day 2: Delhi – Sightseeing Tour

After breakfast, embark on a full-day tour of Old and New Delhi. In Old Delhi, visit the magnificent Red Fort (closed on Mondays), the historic Jama Masjid (17th century largest mosque in India), the bustling market of Chandni Chowk, and Raj Ghat (Mahatma Gandhi's memorial). In New Delhi, drive past the stately India Gate, Parliament House, Secretariat Buildings, and the Vice Regal Palace (now the President's residence). Also visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site Qutub Minar, intended to be the most glorious victory tower in the world and the prototype for all minars in India.

Day 3: Delhi – Jaipur

After breakfast, drive to Jaipur (252 km / 5-6 hours). Upon arrival, check in at your hotel. Jaipur, known as the Pink City, was founded by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1727. It is the only city in the world to symbolize the nine divisions of the Universe through nine rectangular sectors. The city's architecture blends Hindu principles from the Shilpa Shastra with Mughal and Jain influences. Jaipur was painted pink in 1853 to honor Prince Albert's visit, giving it its distinctive appearance. Spend the evening at leisure exploring the local markets.

Day 4: Jaipur – Sightseeing Tour

After breakfast, visit the ancient Rajput capital of Amber Fort. Ascend the fort on elephant back (subject to availability) to see the Diwan-I-Am (Hall of Public Audience), Kali Temple, Jai Mandir (Hall of Victory) with its inlaid panels and mirrored ceiling, and Sukh Niwas (Hall of Pleasure). The fort offers stunning views of the lake below. In the afternoon, tour the Pink City, visiting the Maharaja's City Palace and the Jantar Mantar Observatory, literally meaning "instruments for measuring the harmony of the heavens." Also, take photos at the beautiful Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds) and drive through the pink residential and commercial areas of Jaipur.

Day 5: Jaipur – Agra

After an early breakfast, drive to Agra (240 km / 4-5 hours). Upon arrival, proceed for a tour of Agra's highlights, including the magnificent Taj Mahal. This stunning white marble mausoleum was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The construction began in 1631 and took 22 years to complete, employing 20,000 craftsmen. The Taj Mahal's design combines subtlety with grandeur and immaculate execution with massive scale. Also visit the impressive Agra Fort, started by Emperor Akbar in 1565, with its massive walls stretching 2.5 km and surrounded by a moat over 10 meters wide.

Day 6: Agra – Jhansi – Orchha – Khajuraho

Early breakfast at the hotel before transferring to the railway station. Board the Shatabdi Express train to Jhansi at 8:02 AM, arriving at 10:45 AM. Upon arrival, meet our representative and drive to Khajuraho (189 km / 5-6 hours), stopping en route to visit Orchha. This medieval city features remarkable architecture including the Jahangir Mahal (built in a synthesis of Hindu and Muslim styles), the Raj Mahal with magnificent murals, and the unique Ram Raja Temple (the only palace converted into a temple in India). Also see the Chaturbhuj Temple, laid out in the shape of a cross depicting the four-armed god Krishna, and the Lakshminarayan Temple with well-preserved murals. Continue to Khajuraho and check in at your hotel upon arrival.

Day 7: Khajuraho – Sightseeing – Varanasi

After breakfast, check out and tour Khajuraho's Western Group of temples. The most famous is the 32-meter high Kandariya Mahadev temple with its spectacular sculptures, including sensuous themes. Other temples include the granite Chaunsat Yogini, the Devi Jagdambe temple dedicated to the Mother Goddess, and the Chitragupta temple dedicated to the Sun God Surya. Later, transfer to the airport for your flight to Varanasi. Board Air India flight #405 departing at 13:55 and arriving at 14:40. Upon arrival, meet our representative and transfer to your hotel. (Note: In summer, May to September, this flight operates only on Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday.)

Day 8: Varanasi – Sightseeing

Wake up early for a sunrise boat ride on the sacred Ganges River to witness the bathing ghats and cremation sites. Observing pilgrims performing their rituals at the ghats is one of the most extraordinary experiences in India. Return to the hotel for breakfast. Later, take a city tour of Varanasi, visiting the Bharatmata Temple, Hanuman Temple, and the prestigious Banaras Hindu University campus. In the afternoon, visit Sarnath, where Buddha delivered his first sermon. Tour the ruins, dominated by the Dhamekh Stupa, see the remains of monasteries and the Ashoka Pillar (which once stood over 20 meters high), and visit the Sarnath Museum with its important artifacts including the famous lion capital of the Ashoka Pillar. Return to Varanasi to witness the mesmerizing evening Aarti ceremony at the ghats.

Day 9: Varanasi – Delhi

After breakfast and perhaps some free time to explore more of Varanasi, transfer to the airport for your flight to Delhi. Board Air India flight #AI 405 departing at 16:50 and arriving in Delhi at 18:25. Upon arrival, meet our representative and transfer to your hotel for the final night of your tour.

Day 10: Delhi – Departure

After breakfast, enjoy some free time depending on your flight schedule. At the appropriate time, you will be transferred to the international airport for your departure flight, taking with you unforgettable memories of your journey through India's cultural heartland.
